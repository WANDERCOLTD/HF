# IELTS Writing Task 2 — Essay Types Guide

Source: IDP IELTS official guidance

In IELTS Writing Task 2, you must write an essay of at least 250 words in response to a prompt. This applies to both Academic and General Training modules. You should spend approximately 40 minutes on Task 2.

---

## Essay Type 1: Opinion / Agree-Disagree

### How to Identify

Look for phrases like:
- "To what extent do you agree or disagree?"
- "Do you agree or disagree?"

### Example Prompt

"With traffic jams and pollution increasing every year in big cities, some people say that public transport should be free in big cities to help reduce these problems which are caused by cars. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

### Recommended Structure

1. **Introduction:** Paraphrase the question and clearly state your position
2. **Body Paragraph 1:** First main idea supporting your position, with explanation + examples
3. **Body Paragraph 2:** Second main idea supporting your position, with explanation + examples
4. **Conclusion:** Summarize your position and main points

### Three Position Options

- **Completely agree:** Both body paragraphs support agreement
- **Completely disagree:** Both body paragraphs support disagreement
- **Partially agree:** First paragraph supports agreement; second presents counterpoints

### Key Tips (from ielts.org official guidance)

- Start with brainstorming notes, not immediate writing
- Keep ideas well-developed rather than numerous — "it's much better to have few well-developed ideas than a lot of poorly developed ones"
- Do not add new information in the conclusion
- No more than 4 ideas total across the essay

## Essay Type 2: Discussion

### How to Identify

Prompts contain "Discuss both of these views" plus "give your own opinion."

### Example Prompt

"Some people say that children should learn a foreign language when they are at kindergarten, but others feel that it is better for children to do this when they are teenagers. Discuss both of these views and give your own opinion."

### Recommended Structure

1. **Introduction:** Present both viewpoints and indicate your stance
2. **Body Paragraph 1:** Discuss the first view using phrases like "some claim," "supporters of this view"
3. **Body Paragraph 2:** Discuss the second view with similar distancing language
4. **Conclusion:** Clearly mark your personal position using phrases like "my personal view" or "I am of the belief"

### Key Difference from Opinion Essays

In a discussion essay, you must give fair coverage to BOTH sides before stating your preference. In an opinion essay, you can focus entirely on defending your position.

## Essay Type 3: Two-Part Questions

### How to Identify

Contains two separate questions requiring distinct answers, often using "Why...?" and "How...?" or "What...?"

### Example Prompt

"These days, many cities have problems when they grow quickly, such as accidents and traffic jams. Why do these problems occur? How do these problems impact people who travel for work or study?"

### Recommended Structure

Address each question component systematically with separate development. Each body paragraph typically addresses one of the two questions.

---

## Essay Type 4: Problem-Solution

### How to Identify

Look for phrases like:
- "What are the causes and what solutions can be suggested?"
- "What problems does this cause? What can be done to solve them?"

### Recommended Structure

1. **Introduction:** Paraphrase the problem
2. **Body Paragraph 1:** Causes/problems (2-3 main causes with explanation)
3. **Body Paragraph 2:** Solutions (2-3 practical solutions with explanation)
4. **Conclusion:** Summarize the main cause-solution relationship

Often considered the most straightforward format.

---

## Essay Type 5: Advantages-Disadvantages

### How to Identify

Look for:
- "What are the advantages and disadvantages?"
- "Do the advantages outweigh the disadvantages?"

### Recommended Structure

1. **Introduction:** Paraphrase the topic
2. **Body Paragraph 1:** Advantages (2-3 points with explanation)
3. **Body Paragraph 2:** Disadvantages (2-3 points with explanation)
4. **Conclusion:** State which side outweighs (if asked) or summarize the balance

## Common Task 2 Topics

| Category | Example Topics |
|----------|---------------|
| Education | Classroom learning replacement, free education, early vs late language learning |
| Environment | Economic progress vs sustainability, water pollution, climate action |
| Technology | Screen time effects on children, information access, AI impact |
| Health | Exercise motivation, government vs private healthcare, mental health |
| Society | Parental pressure, rules and laws, urbanisation, work-life balance |
| Crime | Causes of crime, punishment vs rehabilitation |
| Media | Social media influence, advertising to children, news bias |
| Work | Remote working, job satisfaction vs salary, automation |
