# The CIO/CTO Standard — Course References

Three voice-led courses that share one regulated qualification (SIAS / Ofqual *The CIO/CTO Standard* V6.0), one curriculum (5 Units / 26 LOs), one canonical tutor persona, and one 10-skill cross-cutting framework — but split into three deliberately distinct teaching vehicles.

| Course | File | Mode | Duration | Pedagogy | Forward-pointer |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Revision Aid** | `cio-cto-standard-revision-aid.course-ref.md` | coaching-led | 25 min | open scenario per LO → teach next maturity tier → re-ask → silent mastery update | "two LOs grew, one to come back to" |
| **Pop Quiz** | `cio-cto-standard-pop-quiz.course-ref.md` | assessment-led | 10 min | 8–12 MCQs from the Question Bank, one-sentence WHY per answer, no follow-up | "your weakest LO was X — Revision Aid next?" |
| **Exam Assessment** | `cio-cto-standard-exam-assessment.course-ref.md` | discussion-led | 40 min | board-chair persona, 4–6 scenario probes with twists, per-LO per-dimension scoring | "two things to revisit in Revision Aid before re-attempting" |

## Shared anchors (referenced by all three)

- **Verbatim LO source:** SIAS V6.0 — the 26 LO `description` strings are regulated text and must be quoted faithfully. The `performanceStatement` field is the HFF-authored "the learner can…" translation.
- **Canonical persona:** senior CIO mentor. Calm, direct, comfortable with silence. Quotes by Unit name not acronym. Never invents source material. (Exam Assessment shifts the persona into board-chair framing for the session.)
- **10 cross-cutting skills, 4-tier maturity** (Foundation / Developing / Practitioner / Distinction): Stakeholder anticipation, Risk articulation, Commercial framing, Decision velocity, Source-citation discipline, Trade-off explicitness, Stop discipline, Sponsor clarity, Vendor judgement, Operating-cost literacy.
- **5 HFF case studies** (one per Unit): Severn Health Trust (04), Lyle's Brewery (09), Holborn Insurance (10), Polaris Logistics (16), Carrington Foods (21).
- **Single-Unit-per-session** is a hard rule in all three courses.

## How the three relate

- **Pop Quiz** is the routing layer — it surfaces recall gaps and points at Revision Aid.
- **Revision Aid** is the daily practice — it moves the learner up the maturity ladder one LO at a time.
- **Exam Assessment** is the periodic readiness check — it tests judgement under exam conditions and produces structured per-LO per-dimension feedback.

Pop Quiz → Revision Aid → Exam Assessment is the recommended cadence within a Unit. Each course's "What This Course Is NOT" section explicitly redirects boundary cases to the right neighbour.
